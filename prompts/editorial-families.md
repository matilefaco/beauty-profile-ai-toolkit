# Editorial Families

Editorial families are tone directions that help generate varied profile copy while staying grounded in the same facts.

## 1. Warm Professional

Friendly, clear, and polished. Best for service providers who want to sound approachable without being casual.

## 2. Minimal Modern

Short, clean, and direct. Best for profile cards, marketplaces, and professionals with a refined visual style.

## 3. Detail-Focused Craft

Emphasizes technique, care, and precision. Best for specialties where process, finish, and consistency matter.

## 4. Soft Luxury

Elegant and calm without exaggeration. Best for professionals whose client experience is relaxed, elevated, or spa-like.

## 5. Practical Client Guide

Helpful and straightforward. Best for explaining services clearly to clients who may not know technical beauty terms.

## Safety notes

- Tone should never override truthfulness.
- Do not invent premium positioning, certifications, or outcomes.
- Avoid language that pressures clients or creates unrealistic expectations.
- Keep copy inclusive and respectful across hair textures, skin tones, identities, ages, and personal styles.
